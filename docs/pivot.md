## Problem Statement

Iranians who buy from online shops — especially Instagram shops — have no neutral
way to check a shop before they send money. Every trust signal available today
(eNamad seal, Instagram "satisfaction" highlights, comments under posts, follower
count) is **controlled and curated by the seller**. Buyers know this and say so.
The result: money lost to fraud, products that don't match the photos, late or
missing shipments, and shops whose bad reputation never compounds publicly because
the seller deletes the evidence.

The product is still **pre-launch**. The risk is not "v1 isn't finished." The risk
is "we are about to ship a full platform nobody has confirmed they'll use."

---

## Demand Evidence

**Behavioral demand today: still zero.** No paying users, no daily actives, no one
asking when it ships, no one who would scramble if it vanished. Founder confirmed
this honestly: the survey is the first concrete data point.

**The survey (N = 27) is evidence of *problem resonance*, not demand.** It measures
the cheap thing — clicking "5 — I'd use it." Keep that distinction sharp. What the
survey does prove:

- **81% (22/27) scored 4–5** on "would you use a TripAdvisor/Torob-for-shops?" Mean 4.33/5.
- **67% (18/27) discover shops on Instagram.** Reviews today are checked on Instagram
  or the shop's own site — both seller-controlled. No neutral third party exists.
- Trust methods in use are all curatable by the seller: eNamad seal (10), IG
  satisfaction highlights (8), reading comments (7), follower count (1).
- Open-text answers handed us the pitch in users' own words:
  - *pV7xe:* "current trust signals — comments, satisfaction, follower count — are not reliable."
  - *37sRF:* "they delete the negative comments."
  - *hBgnu:* "no mechanism to tell a scam shop from a good one; if all the victims knew of each other, legal pursuit would be easier."
  - *jgNgA:* "the real problem is no serious competitor to Digikala — because there's no trust."

## The landmine (new from this survey)

**56% (15/27) did NOTHING after a bad experience — "too much hassle."** The single
behavior the product depends on (writing reviews / reporting scams) is the one a
majority explicitly refuse to do. Only ~33% vented anywhere: 6 posted on their own
Instagram/Twitter, 3 left complaint comments, 3 reported to cyber police.

Implications, baked into the plan below:
1. **Reports will not flow on their own.** Seed the index yourself so it is never empty.
2. **Lead with the half that delivers value from *looked-up* data, not *contributed* data** — the scam flag works at one data point; a star rating is worthless under ~10 reviews.
3. The ~33% who already vent publicly are the seed contributor pool. Capture them.

## The fraud-vs-disappointment nuance (new from this survey)

The May 28 pivot was built on **fraud** (robbed friends). The survey says the
dominant *lived* pain is **disappointment**, not theft:

- Shipping delay: 10 / 27 (top problem)
- Product doesn't match photos/quality: 7 / 27
- Wrong item: 2 · Poor support after payment: 2 · No problem: 6

Outright fraud appears in the open-text but is not the most common experience. And
while 67% *discover* on Instagram, only ~7 / 27 actually *complete the purchase* on
Instagram — most buy on a website. So the "Instagram fraud" wedge is real but
narrower than the discovery number suggests.

**Decision:** lead with the *scam flag* anyway, because it is the most urgent,
most shareable, and works at zero data — but the schema must hold quality/rating
data from day one so the broader "will this shop disappoint me?" product can turn
on later without a migration.

## Status Quo

When an Iranian considers buying from a shop today: see it on Instagram → try to
vet by scrolling comments, DMing tagged customers, searching the handle on X,
asking a Telegram group, asking a friend → decide on incomplete info → if burned,
complain in a Telegram group or under a post (often deleted), often lose the money.

Cost: real money to fraud, hours of research per purchase, and the same bad shops
keep operating because reputation never compounds publicly.

---

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary user (free side):** Iranian women, 20–40, who shop Instagram pages weekly
(clothing, beauty, home, jewelry). Tech-comfortable, live in Telegram, forward scam
warnings to group chats. Pain is acute — burned, or one DM away from someone who was.

**Secondary user (paying side, later):** honest Instagram shop owners who lose
business because they look identical to scam shops. They'll pay for a verified
badge + claimed page once consumer traffic gives that badge signaling value.

**Narrowest wedge (the entire launch):** a free, no-login page at
`nazarato.com/ig/{handle}` that returns a reputation card for any handle:
- Status badge: **Verified / Unclaimed / Reported**
- Scam-report count + recency, each with an evidence screenshot
- Aggregated review snippets (when they exist)
- A 30-second "report a scam" form (no login, screenshot required)
- A shareable URL, designed to travel in Telegram / X / DMs

Seed it manually before launch from public Telegram scam-warning channels.

## What "make it right" means

Not "build more." The opposite. The current build is a polished v1.0 of "Yelp for
Iran" (PWA, push, voice dictation, blog, owner dashboards, claims, bookmarks,
notifications, settings, admin) — a full platform for an unvalidated model, for
zero users. **Re-aim to the one page.** Everything else becomes a parts bin.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A — Re-aim to the scam-lookup page (Minimal Viable) — RECOMMENDED
Repoint `/shop` + `/write-review` + `/search` at Instagram handles. Add
`/ig/[handle]` reputation card and a 30-second no-login report form. Seed 100 known
handles from Telegram. Hide/disable everything not on the lookup path.
**Effort: S–M (re-aim, not rebuild). Risk: Low.** Reuses ~80% of existing code.

### Approach B — Telegram-bot first (Creative)
Lead with `@nazarato_bot`: send a handle, get a card. Web is the public archive.
**Effort: M–L. Risk: Med.** Higher in-Iran virality, but Telegram platform
dependency, and it discards the polished web UX. Best as a *week-4 distribution
lever on top of A*, not the launch.

### Approach C — Full two-sided launch (scam + ratings + paid badges)
Ship everything. **Effort: XL. Risk: High.** Chicken-and-egg market, empty-ratings
problem, and the delay-until-perfect trap that kills pre-launch founders. Rejected.

---

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A now (weeks 1–3), then Approach B as a launch channel (weeks 4–5).** Web
URL is the artifact every share points at, so it must exist first. Once data exists,
a Telegram bot over the same Supabase backend is ~1 week and multiplies distribution.

---

## Success Criteria — kill/continue gate (4 weeks after launch of A)

**Continue (build the bot) if any TWO are true:**
- 500+ unique handle lookups in week 4 (organic, no paid traffic)
- 20+ user-submitted scam reports *with evidence*
- 5+ unsolicited shares of card URLs in public Telegram / X
- Any unsolicited press, viral post, or channel mention

**Pivot or kill if none happen.** Metric is *behavior that proves the problem is
real and people use the tool* — not "users on the site." Given the 56% landmine,
weight the lookup and share metrics over the report metric.

## Open Questions

- **Legal exposure.** A public "Reported" badge will draw complaints under Iran's
  unpredictable UGC/defamation rules. Require evidence (screenshot/transaction proof)
  on every report from day 1; ship a "shop can respond" flow; add a clear
  "community-reported, not verified by us" disclaimer.
- **Seed sourcing.** Identify the top 3–5 Telegram scam-warning channels; document
  handles with evidence; don't ingest names without it (bad-faith reports will come).
- **Handle normalization.** Handles get renamed/dropped. Snapshot bio + display name
  at first lookup; re-verify weekly. Out of scope for week 1.
- **Persian SEO baseline.** What does `nazarato.com` currently rank for? Quick check.
- **Monetization rails.** Iranian payment (no Stripe) — defer paid badges to month 2+;
  resolve rails (Zarinpal/IDPay/etc.) before charging.
